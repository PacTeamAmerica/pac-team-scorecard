About this scorecard  ·  what it shows and how it works ▼

The Commercial Scorecard gives PAC TEAM a single, living view of where revenue stands across every strategic account: what has been earned, what is committed, and what is still in play.

The bubble at the top frames where 2026 lands as two numbers. Booked + landing is the revenue realistically expected to hit this year: invoiced YTD, plus open orders that aren't on hold, plus pipeline with a PO expected by August 31. The at-risk figure beside it is work that probably slips to 2027: on-hold orders and pipeline without a PO by the cutoff. The Aug 31 line exists because these are build-and-ship projects: if a PO isn't in by then, production can't realistically complete and ship before December 31. The cutoff is automatic. Once the calendar passes Aug 31, not-yet-won pipeline moves to at-risk on its own.

Each account carries one score, built from three layers of decreasing certainty:

  • Invoiced YTD: revenue already recognized this year, net of credit memos, from NetSuite.
  • Open orders: won but not yet billed, the unbilled value of open sales orders.
  • Weighted pipeline: genuine future opportunities from Podio that are not yet in NetSuite, discounted by how likely each is to close: 80%+ counts in full, 50–79% at half, below 50% not at all. Projects already in NetSuite (matched by Project #) are excluded so nothing is double-counted, and PTA and PTNext division projects are excluded, so the pipeline reflects PTJapan and other non-PTA/PTNext work only.

Accounts sit in three tiers: Key accounts (the largest, defended relationships), Strategic growth (accounts with room to scale), and Strategic targets (CEO-approved net-new names). Everything else, including smaller relationships and one-off retail projects such as Diamond Dream Jewelry, rolls up under Other active accounts rather than a strategic tier, so the tiers stay focused on accounts the strategy is actually built around. The active-client count tracks concentration: the strategy favors fewer, deeper relationships, so a smaller number is the goal.

The three layers are kept distinct on purpose: invoiced, open orders, and pipeline never overlap, so each dollar is counted once. Each Monday, three exports (NetSuite invoiced revenue, NetSuite open orders, and the Podio pipeline) are dropped into the panel below and every figure recomputes instantly. Between uploads, the scorecard runs on the last numbers built in, so it always shows something real.
