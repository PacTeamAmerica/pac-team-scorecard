About this scorecard  ·  what it shows and how it works ▼

I built this scorecard to give us one view of where 2026 revenue stands for the PTA business: what we have earned, what is committed, and what is still in play. Each dollar appears in exactly one layer, so nothing is counted twice.

  • Revenue YTD: recognized revenue from NetSuite, the same basis as the P&L.
  • Open orders: work we have won but not yet billed.
  • Weighted pipeline: future opportunity from Podio, discounted by probability. 80%+ counts in full, 50–79% counts at half, below 50% does not count. PTA division only; PTJ and PTNext are booked separately in QuickBooks.

Each account's score is the sum of the three. I added the small bar under each account name to show that score against the account's full 2025 revenue: green means last year is already covered.

The bubble at the top. Booked + landing is what I realistically expect us to hit in 2026: revenue to date, plus open orders not on hold, plus pipeline with a PO expected in time to produce and ship this year. At-risk is work that will likely slip to 2027. The PO deadline depends on the type of work: reorders get more runway than new builds (Packaging reorders until Oct 31, Displays Oct 15, Fixturing Sep 15), everything else uses Aug 31, and Events are judged by their event date. The seasonally adjusted range corrects for our back-loaded year: August and December alone were 42% of 2025 revenue, so a mid-year number always understates where the year lands.

How I keep it current. Each Monday I drop three exports into the panel below (Podio pipeline, NetSuite open orders, NetSuite revenue) and every figure recomputes. Between uploads it runs on the last numbers I built in. I keep accounts in three tiers: Key, Strategic Growth, and Strategic Targets. Everything else rolls up under Other active accounts. The active client count tracks concentration; fewer, deeper relationships is the goal.

Fine print: revenue is P&L recognized revenue (income accounts, net of credit memos). Gross billings run higher because they include freight and duty pass-throughs. About −$179K of non-customer adjustments sits in the company total but not in any account row, so the rows sum slightly higher than the total.
